# FineCheck - Feature Ideas
## Compiled April 2026

---

# 1. SMART TICKET SCANNER

User takes a photo of their parking ticket. AI extracts:
- Fine type (council PCN vs private NtK)
- Operator name
- Date and time of alleged offence
- Contravention code
- Vehicle registration
- Deadline dates

Then automatically routes to the correct appeal process and identifies applicable defences.

**Why it matters:** Most people don't even know if they have a council or private fine. This removes the first barrier. Mobile-first, zero friction.

---

# 2. POFA COMPLIANCE CHECKER

For private parking fines specifically. User enters:
- Date they received the Notice to Keeper (NtK)
- Date of the alleged parking event

AI checks:
- Was the NtK served within 14 days? (If not, keeper liability fails entirely)
- Does the NtK contain the prescribed wording?
- Was a Notice to Driver issued first? (required if driver identified)

This single check could invalidate 20-30% of all private parking fines. Show the result for free as a hook, charge for the full appeal letter.

**Why it matters:** The POFA 14-day rule is the most powerful defence and the one most people don't know about.

---

# 3. SIGNAGE ANALYSER

User uploads photos of the parking signage at the location. AI analyses:
- Is the sign visible from where the car was parked?
- Are the terms clear and unambiguous?
- Does the sign match what the operator claims?
- Are there contradictory signs?
- Is the font size readable? (BPA guidelines specify minimum sizes)

Generates evidence section for the appeal letter.

**Why it matters:** Inadequate signage is the second most successful defence ground after POFA. Photo evidence is powerful.

---

# 4. APPEAL SUCCESS PREDICTOR

Based on accumulated data from user outcomes:
- "PCNs from [operator] at [location] have a 72% appeal success rate"
- "Appeals citing POFA NtK timing succeed 78% of the time"
- "[Council name] concedes 34% of informal challenges"
- "Your specific combination of factors suggests a 65% success probability"

Show the probability for free. Charge for the letter that maximises the chance.

**Why it matters:** Converts hesitant users. "65% chance of saving £100 for £5.99" is a compelling proposition.

---

# 5. OPERATOR INTELLIGENCE DATABASE

For each major parking operator (ParkingEye, UKPC, APCOA, Euro Car Parks, Indigo, NCP, etc.):
- Known issues and common complaints
- Success rates at POPLA/IAS
- Whether they typically pursue County Court
- Their standard response patterns
- Specific weaknesses in their processes
- Whether they use ANPR or physical tickets

Populated from POPLA decisions, tribunal data, forum analysis, and user-submitted outcomes.

**Why it matters:** Generic appeal letters fail. Operator-specific knowledge dramatically improves success rates.

---

# 6. DEADLINE DASHBOARD

User enters their fine details once. Dashboard shows:
- Days remaining for 50% discount
- Days remaining for informal challenge/appeal to operator
- Days remaining for formal representation/POPLA/IAS escalation
- What happens if each deadline passes
- SMS and email reminders at 7 days, 3 days, 1 day before each deadline

**Why it matters:** Many appeals fail because people miss deadlines. This creates ongoing engagement and drives urgency to purchase.

---

# 7. EVIDENCE BUILDER

Guided evidence collection tool:
1. **Photo checklist**: Ticket, signage (all signs), your parking location, any extenuating evidence (broken machines, no signs visible, etc.)
2. **Statement builder**: Structured questions that produce a witness statement
3. **Document upload**: Previous correspondence, receipts, medical evidence
4. **Timeline builder**: Chronological event log with timestamps

All compiled into a professional evidence pack attached to the appeal letter.

**Why it matters:** POPLA/IAS and tribunals are won on evidence. Most people don't know what evidence to collect or how to present it.

---

# 8. COUNCIL LEADERBOARD

Public rankings:
- "Most fines issued per capita" (name and shame)
- "Lowest appeal success rate" (councils that rarely concede)
- "Highest appeal success rate" (councils that are fair)
- "Revenue from parking fines" (public interest data)

Updated from RAC Foundation data, FOI requests, and tribunal statistics.

**Why it matters:** Linkable, shareable content that generates backlinks and press coverage. Journalists love council league tables.

---

# 9. "DID I PARK LEGALLY?" PREVENTIVE CHECK

Before you park, check:
- Enter postcode or location
- See parking restrictions for that area
- CPZ times and zones
- Known problematic private car parks
- User-reported "trap" locations

**Why it matters:** Prevention is better than cure. Creates a reason to return to the site when NOT dealing with a fine. Builds the subscription value.

---

# 10. FLEET DASHBOARD

For businesses with multiple vehicles (delivery companies, sales teams, tradespeople):
- Central view of all fines across all vehicles
- Assign fines to drivers
- Bulk appeal generation
- Monthly analytics (which drivers, which locations, which times)
- Cost tracking and reporting
- Export for accounting

**Why it matters:** Small businesses get multiple fines and waste hours dealing with each one. Aggregation + automation is high value. B2B revenue is typically higher than B2C.

---

# 11. COURT PREP MODULE

For private parking fines that go to County Court small claims:
- Witness statement template
- Defence statement builder
- Key legal arguments (POFA, contractual issues, proportionality)
- What to expect on the day
- "Cheat sheet" of case law references
- Script for verbal defence

**Why it matters:** Most people are terrified of court. A guided, step-by-step module at £29.99 converts well from people who've been served a County Court claim.

---

# 12. APPEAL TEMPLATE LIBRARY

Free, publicly accessible collection of:
- Generic appeal letter templates (good for SEO)
- Operator-specific guides
- Council-specific guides
- Legal reference cards (POFA, TMA, new Code of Practice)
- FAQ per fine type

Premium upgrade: personalised version of any template using their specific details.

**Why it matters:** Free templates are the #1 thing people search for. Capture the traffic, convert to personalised paid letters.

---

# 13. OUTCOME TRACKING & COMMUNITY

After submitting an appeal, users can:
- Update their outcome (won/lost/partial)
- Rate the experience
- Share anonymised details to help others
- See aggregate success rates

Build a community of "successful appellants" who share tips.

**Why it matters:** Creates a feedback loop that improves the AI, builds trust via social proof, and generates engagement.
