## What Does NOT Exist

1. **Automated POFA compliance checker** - Upload photo of Notice to Keeper, AI checks if it was served within 14 days and contains correct wording
2. **Operator-specific AI** trained on each parking company's known weaknesses and successful appeal patterns
3. **Tribunal decision database search** - Searchable database of past decisions to find precedents for your specific situation
4. **Success probability estimator** - "Based on 10,000 similar cases, your appeal has a 67% chance of success"
5. **Deadline tracker with reminders** - Most people miss deadlines because they forget, not because they choose to
6. **Mobile-first photo upload flow** - Take photo of ticket, sign, and location; AI analyses everything
7. **End-to-end tracking** - From initial fine through to resolution, with status updates
8. **Combined council + private** in one tool with correct process routing

# LEGAL FRAMEWORK

## Council PCNs (Penalty Charge Notices)

- Governed by **Traffic Management Act 2004**
- Enforced by local authority civil enforcement officers
- Appeal route: Informal challenge > Formal representation > Traffic Penalty Tribunal (England/Wales) or London Tribunals
- Council CAN register unpaid debt with TEC and send bailiffs
- Clamping/removal possible for persistent evaders

## Private Parking Charges (Parking Charge Notices)

- Governed by **contract law** + **Protection of Freedoms Act 2012** (POFA), Schedule 4
- Issued by private companies (ParkingEye, UKPC, APCOA, Euro Car Parks, etc.)
- Appeal route: Appeal to operator > POPLA (BPA members) or IAS (IPC members)
- Operator CANNOT use bailiffs, clamping, or wheel removal
- Must sue through County Court to enforce (many don't bother for single fines)
- **POFA keeper liability** is the key defence: Notice to Keeper must be served within 14 days of parking event with exact prescribed wording. If not, keeper cannot be held liable and operator must prove who was driving

## New Private Parking Code of Practice (October 2024)

- Caps charges at £100 (£60 if paid in 14 days)
- Mandates 10-minute grace periods
- Requires clear, prominent signage
- New mitigating circumstances charter (fines reduced to £20 for 14 days in some cases)
- Existing sites must comply by late 2026
- Creates fresh confusion and demand for guidance

## Can AI Letters Be Used Legally?

**Yes, completely legal.** No requirement for appeal letters to be written by solicitors. Multiple AI services already operate. The differentiator is personalisation and accuracy of legal arguments, not who/what wrote the letter.
